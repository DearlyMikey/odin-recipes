Chicken Adobo

Description

Chicken Adobo is a classic Filipino dish made by braising chicken in a savory, tangy, and slightly sweet mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns. Often simmered until the meat is tender and infused with rich flavor, it's a comforting, deeply flavorful stew that showcases the balance of salty, sour, and umami typical of Filipino cuisine. Chicken adobo is commonly served over steamed white rice, allowing the bold, aromatic sauce to soak in. It's a staple in Filipino households and considered the unofficial national dish of the Philippines.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil

How to Cook!

  1. Marinate the chicken in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns for at least 30 minutes (or overnight for best flavor).
  2. Sear the chicken in oil over medium heat until lightly browned.
  3. Add the marinade and water to the pot. Bring to a boil.
  4. Lower the heat, cover, and simmer for 30–40 minutes until the chicken is tender.
  5. Uncover and simmer for 10–15 more minutes to reduce and thicken the sauce.
  6. Optional: Add brown sugar to balance the tanginess.
  7. Serve hot over rice.
